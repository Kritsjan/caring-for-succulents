The Basics of Caring for Succulents

Here's what nobody tells you about caring for succulents: they thrive on neglect, but the right kind of neglect. Before diving into advanced care, make sure you've got the fundamentals sorted.

The golden rules I live by:

  • More succulents die from overcare than undercare
  • If in doubt, don't water
  • Bright light is your friend (but introduce it gradually)
  • Well-draining soil isn't optional - it's essential
  • Always use pots with drainage holes
Reality Check: Your succulent doesn't need daily attention. In fact, it'll do better if you check on it weekly at most. Try our watering calculator to create a proper schedule.

Watering: The Key to Success

The most common mistake in succulent care is overwatering. Follow these guidelines:

  • Water thoroughly but infrequently
  • Allow soil to dry completely between waterings
  • Reduce watering in winter months
  • Use well-draining pots with drainage holes

Light Requirements: Finding the Sweet Spot

Most succulents thrive in bright, indirect sunlight. However, their needs can vary:

  • 4-6 hours of bright, indirect sunlight daily
  • Protect from harsh afternoon sun
  • Gradually acclimate to more intense light
  • Watch for signs of sunburn (brown or white patches)

Soil Requirements for Healthy Succulents

The right soil mix is crucial for succulent health. Use a well-draining mixture specifically designed for succulents and cacti. You can create your own mix using:

  • Regular potting soil (40%)
  • Coarse sand or perlite (40%)
  • Small gravel or pumice (20%)

Seasonal Guide to Caring for Succulents

Succulents need different care throughout the year. Here's what to focus on each season:

  • Spring: Perfect time for repotting and fertilizing
  • Summer: Protect from intense afternoon sun
  • Fall: Reduce watering as growth slows
  • Winter: Minimal watering, protect from frost

Common Problems When Caring for Succulents

Leaves Falling Off

Usually overwatering or poor drainage. Check the stem for signs of rot and adjust watering using our calculator.

Stretching or Leaning

Not enough light. Move closer to a window gradually to prevent shock.

Wrinkled Leaves

Actually underwatering (yes, it happens!). Give a good soak but wait for soil to dry before next watering.

FAQ About Caring for Succulents

How do I know if my pot is the right size?

Choose a pot that's about 10% larger than your succulent's root ball. Too large a pot can lead to water retention issues.

Why are my succulent's leaves falling off?

This could be due to overwatering, underwatering, or natural leaf shedding. Check the soil moisture and adjust watering accordingly.

Can I keep succulents indoors?

Yes! Many succulents thrive indoors near bright windows. Just ensure they get enough light and have proper drainage.
